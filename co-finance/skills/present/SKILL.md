## Purpose

Help a student create a clear, well-structured presentation on a finance topic. Produces a slide outline with speaker notes, data visualization suggestions, and delivery tips.

## Input

`$ARGUMENTS` contains the presentation topic or assignment description.

If `$ARGUMENTS` is empty, ask: "What is your presentation about? Include the topic, time limit, audience, and any specific requirements."

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Understand Requirements

Gather: topic, time limit (determines slide count — roughly 1-2 per minute), audience, purpose (inform, persuade, defend, teach), specific requirements, and format (individual or group).

### Step 2 — Design Slide Outline

Create a slide-by-slide outline: Title, Agenda, Context/Background, Main Content (one point per slide), Discussion/Implications, Key Takeaways, Questions (with backup slides), References. Adjust content slides based on time limit.

### Step 3 — Write Speaker Notes

For each slide: what to say (2-3 sentences, not reading the slide), transition to next slide, approximate timing, and an engagement tip (question, pause, example).

### Step 4 — Suggest Data Visualizations

For data slides, recommend chart types:
- Time series: line chart
- Comparison: bar chart
- Composition: pie or stacked bar
- Distribution: histogram
- Relationship: scatter plot
- Geographic: map or heat map

For each: specify what data to include, what to label/highlight, the key takeaway, and source attribution.

### Step 5 — Delivery Tips

- **Opening**: start with a hook — surprising statistic, question, or brief story
- **Pacing**: conversational pace; silence after key points is powerful
- **Slides**: support your narrative, not the narrative itself
- **Data**: tell the audience what to look at in each chart
- **Questions**: repeat the question before answering
- **Time**: practice with a timer; cut content rather than rushing

### Step 6 — Canva Integration (Optional)

If Canva MCP is available, offer to create the presentation directly. Otherwise, suggest building slides from the outline in their preferred tool.
