## What is CO for Finance (COF)?

COF is an AI-powered academic companion for finance students. It helps you research, write, study, and prepare — while you stay in charge of the academic thinking.

**Your job:** Choose your topic, evaluate arguments, form your own analysis, and approve direction before major work begins.

**The AI helps with:** finding/organizing sources, structuring papers and presentations, explaining concepts, generating practice problems, formatting citations, and checking arguments for gaps.

## The Academic Workflow

| Step | Command | What Happens | Your Role |
|------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| 1. Research | `/analyze` | Deep research on your topic | Confirm the direction |
| 2. Planning | `/todos` | Plan deliverables and timeline | Approve the plan |
| 3. Drafting | `/assignment` or `/thesis` | Work section by section | Make the analytical decisions |
| 4. Review | `/challenge` | Stress-test your arguments | Strengthen weak points |
| 5. Save | `/wrapup` | Save progress for next session | Confirm summary |

Plus **`/ws`** anytime to check project status.

## Workspace Resolution

If `$ARGUMENTS` specifies a project name, focus on `workspaces/$ARGUMENTS/`. Otherwise, check for existing workspaces and offer to create one.

## Getting Started

1. **Create a workspace** — ask the AI or create `workspaces/my-project/briefs/`
2. **Describe your assignment** — create a brief describing the assignment, course, deliverable, requirements, and deadline
3. **Run `/analyze`** — kicks off the research phase

If the student already has a workspace, show status with `/ws` instead.

## Other Helpful Commands

`/study` study guide | `/explain` concept explanation | `/practice` practice problems | `/cite` citations | `/formula` formula reference | `/research` literature search | `/ws` project status | `/wrapup` save progress

## Tips

- **Your analysis matters most.** Professors grade your reasoning, not the AI's.
- **You set the direction.** Choose your thesis, pick your framework, decide which evidence is strongest.
- **"I don't understand" is always valid.** Ask for a different explanation anytime.
- **Approval gates protect you.** Never approve a direction you are not comfortable with.
- **Run `/wrapup` before leaving** so the next session picks up where you left off.
- **Academic integrity is your responsibility.** The final work must reflect your own understanding.
