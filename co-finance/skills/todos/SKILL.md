## What This Phase Does

Turn research and outline into a complete project plan — every deliverable, section, and task needed to finish the project, organized by milestone.

## Your Role (Student)

Review and approve the plan before drafting starts. This is your most important checkpoint — the plan is a contract for what gets produced.

## Workspace Resolution

1. If `$ARGUMENTS` specifies a project name, use `workspaces/$ARGUMENTS/`
2. Otherwise, use the most recently modified directory under `workspaces/` (excluding `instructions/`)
3. If no workspace exists, ask the student to create one first
4. Read all files in `workspaces/<project>/briefs/` for student context

## Phase Check

- Read `workspaces/<project>/02-plans/` and `workspaces/<project>/03-structure/` for context
- Check if `todos/active/` already has files (resuming)
- All todos go into `workspaces/<project>/todos/active/`

## Workflow

### 1. Review research and outline

Reference `01-analysis/` and `03-structure/`. Confirm the research foundation is solid and the outline supports drafting. Identify remaining research needs.

### 2. Break down into deliverables

Identify every concrete deliverable: written sections, data work (tables, charts, calculations), citations, supporting materials (appendices, bibliography, abstract), presentation materials (if applicable), and formatting compliance.

### 3. Create task list

Create detailed todos for EVERY task in `todos/active/`.

**CRITICAL: Write ALL todos for the ENTIRE project.** Do NOT limit to "just the first section." Cover every section, data table, citation, and formatting requirement. Each todo should be detailed enough to work on independently. For large projects (20+ todos), organize into numbered milestones.

### 4. Estimate effort and timeline

Map milestones to the deadline. Identify parallel tasks, flag dependencies, and build in time for revision.

### 5. Red team for completeness

Review until no gaps remain: does every outline section have tasks? Are citation and formatting tasks included? Is the timeline realistic? Are there dependency bottlenecks?

### 6. STOP — present plan and wait for approval

Present the complete plan by milestone. For each: what will be completed, how many tasks, dependencies, and timeline. Then ask:

1. "Does this cover everything your assignment requires?"
2. "Is anything here that you do not need?"
3. "Is anything missing?"
4. "Does the order make sense?"
5. "Does the timeline work with your deadline?"

**Do NOT proceed to drafting until the student explicitly approves.**
